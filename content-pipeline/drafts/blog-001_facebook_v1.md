If you've built an AI workflow that works fine once but starts behaving strangely over multiple steps — the issue is usually not the model.

It's that the workflow has no control flow.

AI systems that handle multi-step tasks need a loop structure: Think (plan the next move), Act (execute one thing), Verify (check the result before continuing). That last part — verify before moving forward — is what most setups skip.

The other thing that gets skipped: a stop condition. Without one, the system either keeps running after it's done, or stops too early and looks finished when it isn't.

This isn't about building complex agents. It's about understanding why "I set up an AI automation" and "I have a reliable AI automation" are different statements.
