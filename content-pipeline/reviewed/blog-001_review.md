## LinkedIn — blog-001_linkedin_v1.md

**What works:**
- Opens with a concrete distinction, no warm-up sentence
- Three-phase breakdown is operational and directly traceable to source
- Final sentence is the strongest claim in the post: "Most AI automations that behave unpredictably aren't missing a better model. They're missing control flow." — specific, non-generic, verifiable from source
- Length is correct (~220 words)
- No rejected patterns present

**What feels weak:**
- "Here's what the three phases actually do:" — the word "actually" is mild performative framing. Reads slightly like setup for a revelation that isn't a revelation. Could just be removed.
- Bold **Think** / **Act** / **Verify** header structure risks looking like a LinkedIn listicle. Acceptable here because the content is operational, but worth watching in future posts.
- "without losing the thread halfway through" — vague metaphor. Doesn't add signal.

**What still sounds AI-generated:**
- Nothing that would trigger rejection. The listicle-adjacent structure is the only latent risk.

**Verdict:** Pass. Minor edits optional, not required.
**Suggested for V2:** Move the final sentence to the opening, restructure from there.

## Instagram — blog-001_instagram_v1.md

**What works:**
- First line holds up in feed ("An AI that answers is a tool. An AI that loops is a system.")
- Three-phase breakdown is visual-ready — tight, scannable
- Hashtag count correct (7)
- Stop condition insight is retained

**What feels weak:**
- Final paragraph: "This is the structure behind every reliable AI agent. Not a framework. Not a platform. Just: think, act, check, repeat until done." — this construction is tired. "Not a framework. Not a platform." is an increasingly common LinkedIn/IG rhetorical move. It sounds like content, not like thought.
- Uses "check" instead of "verify" — inconsistency with source terminology. Small but matters for brand precision.
- Visual direction note is mixed into the caption block. It should be separated more clearly — reviewer needs to know what is copy vs. production notes.

**What still sounds AI-generated:**
- The closing paragraph. It has the "landing statement" rhythm of auto-generated content: restate the concept, add a contrast, close with a summary phrase. It should be cut or replaced.

**Verdict:** Conditional pass. Final paragraph requires rewrite before approval.
**Suggested for V2:** Cut final paragraph. End on "The control flow is." or add one concrete sentence instead of a summary.

## Facebook — blog-001_facebook_v1.md

**What works:**
- Opens with a scenario ("works fine once but starts behaving strangely over multiple steps") — concrete, reader-recognizable, doesn't assume technical knowledge
- Explanation is accessible without being dumbed-down
- The "I set up an AI automation" vs "I have a reliable AI automation" distinction is clean and quotable
- Correct hashtag count (3), correct link placement

**What feels weak:**
- Opening sentence is slightly long. "If you've built an AI workflow that works fine once but starts behaving strangely over multiple steps — the issue is usually not the model." — works, but could be split.
- "This isn't about building complex agents." — defensive framing. The author is managing a hypothetical objection rather than making a statement. Weakens the confidence of the post.
- Transition from explanation to "we wrote about the full structure on the blog" is abrupt. One bridging sentence is missing.

**What still sounds AI-generated:**
- "This isn't about building complex agents." The defensive pre-emptive objection handling is a known AI writing pattern. Real operators don't usually clarify what the post isn't about.

**Verdict:** Conditional pass. Two targeted edits needed.
**Suggested for V2:** Remove defensive sentence. Add one bridging sentence before the outro.
