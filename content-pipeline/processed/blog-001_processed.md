## Core message

An AI that answers is a tool. An AI that loops is a system.
The difference is control flow: rules that determine when to continue, stop, backtrack, or escalate.

## Audience

Operators and builders who use AI tools or are building AI-assisted workflows.
Small business owners who have set up some form of AI automation but don't understand why it behaves unpredictably.

## Operational insights

1. Think → Act → Verify is not a technique. It is the minimum structure for completing multi-step tasks reliably.
2. "One action = one move" — this constraint is what makes a loop controllable. Breaking it causes unpredictable behavior.
3. The most common missing piece is the stop condition. Without it, a system loops forever or exits prematurely.
4. Control flow answers four questions: when to continue, when to stop, when to backtrack, when to escalate.
5. Without control flow, a system wanders. With it, behavior is stable and repeatable.
