# LinkedIn Visual Assets

## What works

- **Text cards (Category A):** Clean dark background, short claim or framework. Works when the concept is structurally simple.
- **Sketch diagrams (Category C):** Process maps, loops, decision flows. Works when the post describes a system architecture.
- **Operational screenshots (Category B):** Real workflow, terminal, or tool screenshot with minimal annotation.
- **No image:** LinkedIn text posts are viable. If the post is strong enough, no image is better than a weak image.

## Aspect ratios

- `1200 × 627` (1.91:1) — standard link/image preview, appears large in feed
- `1080 × 1080` (1:1) — square, clean, no cropping risk
- Avoid portrait formats on LinkedIn — feed renders them awkwardly

## Pairing logic

LinkedIn captions are long-form. The visual supports the post but does not need to carry it.

- Post with phase breakdown → diagram of the loop, not a quote card repeating the phases
- Post with a sharp single claim → Category A typography card with that claim
- Post with a process lesson → operational screenshot or sketch map

The visual does not restate the caption. It frames it.

## What to avoid

- Carousel format (LinkedIn carousels are PDF uploads — fine in theory, but complex to produce correctly at this stage)
- Motivational typography
- Centered full-bleed quote with gradient background
- Anything that looks like it belongs on Instagram
