# Tone Guide

## Metaxis Voice

**Direct.** Say the thing. No warm-up sentences.
**Calm.** No urgency theater. No "you need to read this."
**Precise.** Concrete nouns over abstract concepts.
**Slightly ironic.** Aware of the gap between how things are described and how they actually work.
**Practical.** Grounded in operations, not theory.
**High-trust.** Assumes the reader is intelligent and busy.
**Human.** Not performed warmth. Not AI cheerfulness.

---

## What Metaxis Tone Sounds Like

- "This is what the system does and why we built it that way."
- "Most decision frameworks fail at step 3. Here's the fix."
- "We automated the repetitive part. The judgment call stayed human."
- "Build the process first. Then decide if it needs a tool."
- "AI is a useful assistant. It is not a strategy."

---

## What Metaxis Tone Does NOT Sound Like

**Hype:**
> "AI is revolutionizing the way small businesses operate."

**Fake urgency:**
> "If you're not using these 5 AI tools, you're already behind."

**Guru cadence:**
> "Most people don't realize this one thing holds them back..."

**Empty authority:**
> "As a systems thinker, I've learned that..."

**Corporate warmth:**
> "We're passionate about empowering entrepreneurs to reach their full potential."

**AI-assistant cheerfulness:**
> "Great question! Here's what you need to know about..."

---

## Calibration Test

Read the draft aloud.

Ask:
1. Would a skeptical, experienced operator find this credible?
2. Does it contain a specific, verifiable claim?
3. Does it avoid performance of expertise?
4. Could this be said without the brand name and still be recognizable as Metaxis?

If no to any of these: rewrite.

---

## Tone Is Not

- Coldness
- Arrogance
- Minimalism for its own sake
- Jargon as a substitute for clarity

Tone is consistent signal. The reader should know who is talking after two sentences.
