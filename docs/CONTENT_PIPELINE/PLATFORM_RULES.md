# Platform Rules

LinkedIn, Instagram, and Facebook are not the same channel.
Same content posted to all three is a signal of a broken pipeline.

---

## LinkedIn

**Audience:** Professionals, operators, builders, decision-makers.

**Format:**
- Opens with a specific observation or concrete claim (not a question hook)
- 150–400 words optimal
- Short paragraphs, single-line breaks
- No hashtag spam — 2–4 relevant tags maximum
- Links are acceptable (they suppress reach; still use them when relevant)

**Tone:** Professional but human. Direct. Slightly dry.

**Works well:**
- Build logs, process breakdowns, lessons from real work
- "Here's what we built and why" format
- Decision frameworks with operational context

**Fails hard:**
- Motivational "thought leadership"
- Carousel-style bullet dumps
- Asking questions to drive comments ("What do you think?")
- Soft inspirational stories without operational point

---

## Instagram

**Audience:** Visually oriented, shorter attention, context established by profile.

**Format:**
- Caption: 100–200 words for educational content, can be shorter for visual-led posts
- First line must carry full weight — feed shows only ~125 characters before "more"
- 5–8 hashtags, placed after content (not embedded in copy)
- No external links in caption (bio link only)

**Tone:** Calm, clear, slightly editorial. Not casual, not corporate.

**Works well:**
- Single sharp insight per post
- Visual-led content with caption as context
- Carousel with 5–7 slides covering one concrete idea
- Process diagrams, system maps, short frameworks

**Fails hard:**
- Long-form reasoning (belongs on LinkedIn)
- Generic inspiration
- Hashtag stuffing
- Captions that only describe the image

---

## Facebook

**Audience:** Mixed — depends heavily on Page audience composition.
For Metaxis: professional/SMB segment, likely older than IG audience.

**Format:**
- 100–300 words
- Can include external link with context sentence
- 0–3 hashtags (Facebook hashtag discovery is weak)
- Questions are acceptable only when genuinely open and relevant

**Tone:** Slightly warmer than LinkedIn. Still direct, not casual.

**Works well:**
- Summarizing blog content with link to read more
- Short process explanations with practical outcome
- Community-relevant questions about real operational problems

**Fails hard:**
- Engagement bait ("comment YES if you agree")
- Copy-pasted IG caption
- Posts that assume feed algorithm will do the work

---

## Cross-Platform Rule

Do not post identical content across platforms.
Each platform requires a rewrite, not a copy-paste.
The core idea can be the same. The format, length, and tone must differ.
